On the Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies (1905)

Abstract: Einstein proposes a new theory of space and time based on two postulates: the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames, and the speed of light is constant regardless of the motion of the source or observer. This paper eliminates the need for the concept of “aether” and introduces time dilation, length contraction, and the relativity of simultaneity.

“The introduction of a ‘luminiferous ether’ will prove to be superfluous… The theory presented here reconciles Maxwell’s equations with the principle of relativity.”
