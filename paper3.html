On a Heuristic Point of View Concerning the Production and Transformation of Light (1905)

Abstract: Einstein suggests that light consists of discrete packets of energy—later called photons—and explains how these quanta can eject electrons from metal surfaces. This paper laid the foundation for quantum theory and earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.

“According to the assumption considered here, when a light ray is absorbed by a material, the energy is absorbed in discrete quanta.”
