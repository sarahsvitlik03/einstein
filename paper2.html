Does the Inertia of a Body Depend Upon Its Energy Content? (1905)

Abstract: This short paper introduces the famous equation \( E = mc^2 \), showing that mass and energy are interchangeable. Einstein argues that if a body emits energy \( L \) in the form of radiation, its mass decreases by \( L/c^2 \).

“If a body gives off the energy \( L \) in the form of radiation, its mass diminishes by \( L/c^2 \). The mass of a body is a measure of its energy content.”
